SDO Sorsogon Week 1

Getting Acquainted at DepEd Sorsogon

We spent the first week learning our assigned schools and familiarizing ourselves with the tasks ahead.

During the first week of our OJT at the Department of Education (DepEd) Schools Division of Sorsogon, we were assigned to various branches of the office, where we learned about the specific roles and responsibilities of each department. Our tasks included hands-on activities such as encoding, understanding the basics of LAN cabling, and other fundamental IT-related duties to get familiar with the office's technological needs and workflow. This week set the foundation for our learning experience throughout the internship.

SDO Sorsogon Week 2

Website Updates and Content Management

Focusing on redesigning the website using WordPress and updating key content like memos, issuances, and special orders.

During Week 2 of our OJT at DepEd Sorsogon, we worked on redesigning the official website using WordPress, enhancing its layout and user experience. Our tasks also involved updating various content, including memos, issuances, special orders, and other important documents. This hands-on experience allowed us to apply our web design skills and learn how to manage and maintain official online content, contributing to the accessibility and functionality of the website for the community.

SDO Sorsogon Week 3

Website Maintenance and Acquaintance Party Performance

In our final week at DepEd Sorsogon, we continued updating the website and its content, while I also performed as a guitarist at The Lewis College acquaintance party.

During the final week of my OJT at DepEd Sorsogon, we continued updating and maintaining the website, ensuring that key documents and content were current. In addition to our technical tasks, I had the opportunity to perform as a guitarist at The Lewis College acquaintance party, showcasing my musical skills while engaging with fellow students and staff. This week was a blend of both professional development and personal enjoyment, marking the conclusion of my internship experience in SDO Sorsogon.

Sorsogon East Central School Week 4

IT Support and Performing Arts Judging

We transferred to Sorsogon East Central School (SECS), where we focused on IT tasks, and I also judged the performing arts auditions at The Lewis College.

We transitioned to Sorsogon East Central School (SECS), where we were briefed on our new tasks, which included fixing computers, laptops, printers, encoding data, and handling LAN cabling. This week also saw me stepping into a different role as one of the judges for The Lewis College's performing arts auditions, blending my technical skills with a creative involvement in the school’s activities. It was a dynamic week of both IT troubleshooting and contributing to the college's vibrant cultural events.

Sorsogon East Central School Week 5

LAN Cabling and Network Setup

We replaced and managed all LAN cables at SECS, and I was responsible for setting up the school’s network.

During Week 5 of our OJT at SECS, we focused on replacing and managing the LAN cables for all the computers in the school. We organized and tidied up the cables to ensure a cleaner, safer, and more efficient setup. Additionally, I was tasked with setting up the school's network, ensuring proper connectivity across all devices. This week provided hands-on experience with network infrastructure and reinforced the importance of well-maintained systems for smooth IT operations.

Sorsogon East Central School Week 6

Student Reading Profiling and Distribution

I created reading profiles for students from kindergarten to grade 6 using Excel, and distributed the printed profiles to each classroom.

I was assigned the task of creating reading profiles for all students from Kindergarten to Grade 6. Using Excel, I organized the data and generated the profiles, which were then printed out. My responsibility also included distributing the printed profiles to every classroom, ensuring that each teacher had the necessary information to support their students' learning needs. This task enhanced my data management skills and taught me about the importance of accurate record-keeping in the educational process.

Sorsogon East Central School Week 7

Network Maintenance and LAN Connection Fixes

I performed maintenance work on the network and fixed LAN connections for computers that were not receiving internet access.

I focused on network maintenance to resolve issues with LAN connections. Some computers were experiencing connectivity problems and had no internet access. I was tasked with troubleshooting and resetting up the connections to restore proper network functionality. This week allowed me to refine my problem-solving and technical skills, ensuring that the school's IT systems were running smoothly and efficiently for both staff and students.

Sorsogon East Central School Week 8

Teacher’s Conference Support and IT Maintenance

We provided IT support for a teacher's conference, ensuring the projector worked properly, creating presentation slides, and handling other technical tasks.

We were invited to provide IT support for a teacher's conference. Our tasks included ensuring the projector worked smoothly, preparing the presentation slides, and handling any other IT-related needs during the event. I was responsible for making sure everything ran without technical issues, from equipment setup to troubleshooting on the spot. This experience gave me valuable insight into event coordination from a tech perspective, enhancing my skills in IT support for live events.

Sorsogon East Central School Week 9

Ongoing IT Maintenance and Support

In Week 9, we continued with regular maintenance work, troubleshooting issues, and ensuring the smooth operation of the school's IT systems.

I was involved in routine maintenance work across the school's IT systems. This included troubleshooting hardware and software issues, ensuring all computers were functional, and verifying that network connections were stable. We also conducted preventive maintenance, checking and cleaning devices to minimize future problems. This week focused on maintaining the overall functionality of the school's IT infrastructure, ensuring that both staff and students had reliable access to the technology they needed for daily activities.

Sorsogon East Central School Week 10

System Finalization and Pre-Oral Defense Preparation

Finalizing our system and documents in preparation for the upcoming pre-oral defense, while continuing with maintenance work.

Continuing with regular maintenance work on the school’s IT systems, ensuring everything was functioning smoothly. This week also marked an important milestone as we met with our advisers to finalize our system and documents in preparation for the upcoming pre-oral defense. We reviewed and polished our work, making sure everything was ready for presentation. This combination of technical maintenance and academic preparation helped us stay on track for both our internship and academic goals.

Sorsogon East Central School Week 11

UPS and AVR Replacement, New Computer Setup

I checked the storage room for working UPS and AVR units to replace the old ones and set up new computers for the school.

I was tasked with checking the storage room for any working UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) or AVR (Automatic Voltage Regulator) units to replace the outdated ones. After locating the replacements, I ensured they were properly installed to improve the power management and protection of the school’s IT equipment. Additionally, I was responsible for setting up new computers, ensuring they were configured and ready for use by the staff and students. This week focused on upgrading and maintaining the school’s IT infrastructure for better performance and reliability.

Sorsogon East Central School Week 12

Printer Maintenance and Final Troubleshooting

The final week of our OJT, we focused on fixing and troubleshooting printers, performing cleaning, and conducting deep maintenance tasks.

During the last week of my OJT at Sorsogon East Central School (SECS), we were assigned to perform maintenance on the printers throughout the school. This involved troubleshooting any issues, fixing malfunctions, and ensuring that all printers were running smoothly. We also carried out routine cleaning and deep cleaning tasks to maintain the machines' longevity and efficiency. This week was a hands-on conclusion to our technical duties, ensuring the school's equipment was fully operational as we wrapped up our internship.
